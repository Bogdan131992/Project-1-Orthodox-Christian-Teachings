"Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God"

(Romans 10,17)

The need to know and practice one's own belief

Faith is a gift from God, this gift must be worked on and increased to help us get to know God better and experience Him in a real way through his grace.

Indifference and ignorance are considered sins, because they separate people from God, and thus people forget their purpose on this earth and lose the possibility of spiritual growth necessary to acquire love for God and all humans, qualities necessary for the eternal salvation of the soul created after in the Image and Likeness of God.

The path of faith is a chance to grow in a personal relationship with God. A relationship that God showed us that he wants by sending his Son to earth to teach us everything about faith and who sacrificed Himself by giving His life for us on the cross.

Seeing that many believers, even though they are baptized, no longer know their own faith, but have the desire to find more about it, we decided to help them by organizing orthodox catechesis in our Orthodox Church from the german town Leipzig

The catechesis will be organized and presented by the parish priest together with some Orthodox theology graduates from our parish.

If you are interested, please fill out the form that you finde it on the registration page and send it to us, then we will send you a confirmation email with all the information you need to attend our meetings.

Our meetings take place in the Orthodox Church from Leipzig, Germany.


  • Every Thursday from 6:00 p.m.

  • Adress: Russenstr. 194, 04288 Leipzig.

  • We start with a small prayer before every meeting
  • We will have twelve meetings in groups of 20-30 people, for three different age groups.
  • In each meeting, the priest or catechists will present a theme for about an hour
  • Followed by half an hour of dialogue with questions and answers
  • In the end will enjoy a small agape
  • Priority geven by order of registration!
  • Don't forget to bring a pen, notebook and a Bible!
